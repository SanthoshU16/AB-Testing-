ARMOR BRIDZ COOKIE POLICY

To provide a functional and secure phishing simulation platform, we use a minimal set of cookies and similar tracking technologies.

This policy describes what cookies we use, why we use them, and how you can control your preferences. We are committed to transparency and privacy in every interaction.

1. About Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit a website. They help the website recognize your device and remember certain information about your visit or preferences.

On the Armor Bridz platform, we use cookies primarily for security, authentication, and to accurately track the results of authorized phishing simulations.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies and tracking technologies for the following purposes:

  • Authentication: To keep you signed in to the admin dashboard and ensure your session remains secure.
  • Security: To protect our infrastructure and prevent unauthorized access or malicious activity.
  • Simulation Performance: To detect when an employee opens a simulation email or clicks a link, which is essential for risk assessment.
  • Preferences: To remember your interface settings, such as your theme or language choices.

3. Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the platform to function. You cannot opt out of these cookies as they are required for security and session management:

  • Firebase Authentication: We use Google Firebase to manage admin login sessions. These tokens are stored securely in your browser's local storage or cookies.
  • Session Management: Temporary cookies used to maintain your interaction with the admin panel during a single visit.
  • CSRF Protection: Security cookies used to prevent cross-site request forgery attacks.

4. Simulation Tracking Technologies

Because Armor Bridz is a phishing simulation platform, we use specific tracking mechanisms within simulation campaigns:

  • Tracking Pixels: Transparent 1x1 images included in simulation emails to detect when the email is opened.
  • Unique Link Identifiers: Parameters added to links within simulation emails to identify which employee clicked the link and at what time.
  • Fingerprinting (Minimal): We may collect browser version and device type to help organizations identify vulnerable software versions within their workforce.

Note: These tracking technologies are only active for employees enrolled in an authorized simulation by their organization. No tracking is performed on public-facing pages for marketing purposes.

5. Your Choices & Controls

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • Clear cookies from your browser at any time
  • Block all cookies (note: this will prevent the Armor Bridz admin panel from functioning)
  • Set your browser to notify you when you receive a cookie

For more information on how to manage cookies, visit aboutcookies.org.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy as we add new features or as security standards evolve. Any material changes will be reflected on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date.

Our use of cookies is focused entirely on security and platform functionality. We do not use advertising cookies or share your browsing data with third parties.

If you have questions about our use of tracking technologies, please contact your organization's security administrator or our support team.
